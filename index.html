Intro

If I had to describe Gautam in two words, he'd be a "Thoughtful Builder".
Sounds understated until you see the care he puts into everything he ships.
Sounds simple until you watch him rebuild something from scratch just because it didn't feel right the first time.

On the rare occasions when you don't catch him buried in a codebase or a design rabbit hole, you can find him at the gym, lost in music, or in the middle of a conversation that ran way longer than planned.

Gautam grew up in the vibrant city of Jaipur (India) and proudly calls it home. Driven by an itch to build things that actually matter, he pursued Computer Science at Bennett University — where curiosity met code and never really looked back.

In the words of Gautam:

I've always been a tinkerer. As a kid, I took things apart not to break them — but to understand them. That instinct never left. It just found a better outlet in code.

I don't believe in doing both at once — when I'm building a frontend, I'm fully in the craft of the interface, the feel, the detail. When I'm on the backend, I'm thinking in systems, structure, and clean logic. Going all-in, one layer at a time — that's how I work.

I chose Computer Science because I wanted to build things that didn't exist yet. That feeling — of turning an idea into something real and working — is what gets me out of bed every single day.
As Steve Jobs once said:
"People who are crazy enough to think they can change the world are the ones who do"
